About the Firm

Dedicated Exclusively to Special Education Litigation

Jacobson Law is a New York–based practice focused solely on enforcing the educational rights of children with disabilities through disciplined litigation strategy.

This practice was founded by Hugh Jacobson, an experienced New York attorney who previously built and led an established law firm focused on complex legal matters across multiple areas of law. Drawing on that background, Mr. Jacobson founded this firm to apply disciplined legal strategy to special education matters — where enforcement, precision, and preparation directly affect children's futures.

Litigation-Driven Advocacy for Children's Educational Rights

Our work is grounded in careful case analysis, evidence-driven strategy, and clear execution. We do not operate as advisors navigating the process or rely on persuasion alone. We litigate to enforce legal obligations and secure meaningful educational access for children with disabilities.

We approach each matter with structure and control, building cases around evaluations, expert analysis, and a complete factual record. This allows us to deliver consistent outcomes in high-impact cases while maintaining the standards expected of a serious litigation practice.

What We Do

How We Enforce
Educational Rights

IEP Analysis & Enforcement

We identify failures in your child's IEP — wrong placements, missing services, unsupported goals — and take action to enforce compliance.

Behavioral & Program Failures

When schools fail to assess behaviors or implement effective plans, we build the case to demand accountability and proper support.

Independent Evaluations

We secure and deploy independent evaluations to reveal your child's true needs and expose gaps in district assessments.

Due Process Litigation

We litigate IDEA cases from filing through decision — with disciplined preparation, expert testimony, and focused legal advocacy.

Compensatory Services

We pursue meaningful remedial education — including therapy, specialized instruction, and extended services — tailored to remedy educational loss.

Accountability

We ensure court-ordered services are funded, delivered, and implemented — and pursue enforcement when districts fail to comply.
